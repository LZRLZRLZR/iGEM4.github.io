HUMAN PRACTICE

Overview

Our project was focused on tacking the problem of the Samonellosis in the poultry industry, which is tightly related to the society. To bridge the technological gap between the i-GEMers and industry, we gained advice from both professionals and stakeholders related to the production chain. Through integrated human practices, we hope to realize the following goals with in-time feedbacks:

  • √ An elaborate design for experiments
  • √ A convenient, farmers-oriented product
  • √ A responsibility to gurantee the safety
  • √ A future plan for further application & promotion

To make sure all the factors have been taken into consideration, we develop a triangle model to categrize our human practice process——technology, economy&ecology, and society. Armed with this we can infuse our HP trees with possible branches.

As we are designing an effective, highly specific killing mechanism of our E.coli, an article, Engineered toxin–intein antimicrobials can selectively target and kill antibiotic-resistant bacteria in mixed population, inspired us. Thus, we dig deeper in these three points:
 How to avoid toxicity to host
 How to maximize the killing-effect
 How to be specific towards ABR-Salmonella
Later, we found OrtT ( a toxin) to replace bacteriocin, because Salmonella’s responding bacteriocin is outer cellular and plant-based.

Institute for Food and Drug Control and related administration

 If the remains in chicken excreta should meet standard?

We want our products to be widely used in the industry. So, we may contact both traditional media and emerging social media. We plan to hold a meet-up with stakeholders, to negotiate future promotion, as well as be reported by media. We will consult professionals on the communication and marketing management.

As we start the project, we are determined to center on the research related to Antibiotic Resistance, which is meaningful and challenging. So we plan to consult Pro. Zhou researching on Antibiotic Resistance to have a big-picture viewpoint of the situation. Besides, we want to explore the public knowledge of Antibiotics and bacterial diseases, also as a part of public engagement.

Given the husbandary contributes to the most of the overuse of antibiotics, we will call at Poutry Institute, Veterinarian to confirm our subject animals and relative disease, and then consult Pro. Zou focusing on chicken feed. And then, we move to design the project. To tackle the potential bioethical problem in the project, we will consult a bioethical expert, for example, how we should design and test our product according to the principle of No Release.

When we move to design the project, we will interview different participants in the production chain. Hennery, the most important one, and from the upstream feed suppliers to the downstream processing plant.

After we have cultivated our engineering bacteria, we expect to find the best and safe carrier of our antibacterial drug, as well as the potential eqquipment to deliver our drug, so we consult the Ropeedar Feed Company hope that the microcapsule can be the best carrier.

As we test the final effect, we will ask for the feedback from our target customers—farmers in the hennery. Meanwhile, we hope to get the recognation of Institute for Food and Drug Control, related administration for our future promotion.

Besides, we aim to offer a user-friendly service to our customers, including the instructions of our Prickly Chicky, relevant training programme, and its matched software to control the amount of the microcapsule.
