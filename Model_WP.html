Overview

In order to learn about the metabolic expression of Salmonella after conjugation and the killing effect of bacteria, the two parts were modeled respectively:

  • Internal
  • External

Internal

The metabolism of Salmonella after successful conjugation mainly includes transcription, translation, splicing of DnaE and degradation. We use a top-down approach to find the balance of this process.

pic1.Salmonella Genetic Circuit

This model mainly includes two parts, one is the control system of Salmonella species specificity and tetracycline resistance specificity, the other is the expression system of ortT.

1) Specific control system: T7 RNase can only start transcription in cells with tetracycline resistant gene, while T3 RNase can only start transcription in Salmonella cells which has SsrB in their cells.

2) OrtT expression system: T7 RNase starts the transcription of the N-terminal of toxin protein OrtT and peptide linker DnaE, while T3 RNase starts the transcription of the C-terminal of toxin protein OrtT and peptide linker DnaE. They are translated and then further splices to form mature OrtT.

Parameter Estimation

Transcription:

Generally, the transcription rate is 500～4000bp / min, while T7 RNase gene is 2751bp, T3 RNase is 2652bp, OrtT-N with DnaE-N is 447bp, DnaE-C with OrtT-C is 195bp. Based on this, it is roughly estimated that the transcription rate of T7 RNase and T3RNase is 0.02/s, that of OrtT-N is 0.029/s, and that of OrtT-C is 0.067/s

Because T7 promoter needs to combine with T7 RNase to initiate transcription, T3 promoter also needs to combine with T3 RNase to initiate transcription, Hill function is used to simulate the interaction of DNA and T7 RNase during the process.${\theta} = \frac{{[L]}^n}{{K_d}^n + [L]^n} = \frac{1}{1+(\frac{K_{d}}{[L]})^{n}}$

As T3 RNase and T7 RNase are activators, we are interested in how much of the DNA is bound and active:$\frac{1}{1+(\frac{K_{d}}{[Activator]})^{n}}$ .Here we estimate Kd as 3.7, n as 1.

A shutdown promoter still has a very small transcription which is called the leakiness of the promoter. In our model the leakiness rate is estimated to be 3.077×10^(-5)/s.

Translation: The translation speed of protein is about 3-7aa / s. We also estimate the translation rate according to the length of gene. The translation speed of T7 RNase and T3 RNase are 0.005/s, that of OrtT-N with DnaE-N is 0.029/s, and that of OrT-N with DnaE-N is 0.067/s.

Peptide splicing:

According to the known literature, the splicing rate of the peptide is 0.011/s, and the de splicing rate is 6.87×10^(-4)/s.

Degradation:

Due to the high degradation rate of mRNA and relatively low degradation rate of protein, the roughly estimated degradation rate of mRNA and protein in the model is 0.002/s and 0.0001/s respectively.

Therefore, the whole metabolism process of Salmonella cells is as follows:

$$\begin{align} \frac{{\large d} m_{T7RNase}}{d t}&=（L_{TRE}+\alpha_1） {\cdot} T7RNase_{gene} – d_{mT7} {\cdot} m_{T7RNase} \\ \frac{{\large d} m_{T3RNase}}{d t} &=（L_{SPI-2}+\alpha_2） {\cdot} T3RNase_{gene} – d_{mT3} {\cdot} m_{T3RNase}\\ \frac{{\large d}{T7RNase}}{d t}&=\beta_1 {\cdot} {m_{T7RNase}} - d_{T7RNase} {\cdot} {T7RNase}\\ \frac{{\large d}{T3RNase}}{d t}&=\beta_2 {\cdot} {m_{T3RNase}} - d_{T3RNase} {\cdot} {T3RNase}\\ \frac{{\large d} m_{OrtTN}}{d t}&=(L_{T7}+{\frac{\alpha_3}{1+({\frac{K_{d}}{T7RNase}})^{n}}}) {\cdot} OrtTN_{gene} - d_{mOrtTN}{\cdot}m_{OrtTN}\\ \frac{{\large d} m_{OrtTC}}{d t}&=(L_{T3}+{\frac{\alpha_4}{1+({\frac{K_{d}}{T3RNase}})^{n}}}) {\cdot} OrtTC_{gene} - d_{mOrtTC}{\cdot}m_{OrtTC}\\ \frac{{\large d} {OrtTN}}{d t}&=\beta_3 {\cdot} {m_{OrtTN}}-k_1{\cdot}{OrtTN}{\cdot}{OrtTC}+k_{-1}{\cdot}{OrtT} – d_{OrtTN}{\cdot}{OrtTN}\\ \frac{{\large d} {OrtTC}}{d t}&=\beta_4 {\cdot} {m_{OrtTC}}-k_1{\cdot}{OrtTN}{\cdot}{OrtTC}+k_{-1}{\cdot}{OrtT} – d_{OrtTC}{\cdot}{OrtTC}\\ \frac{{\large d} {OrtT}}{d t}&=k_1{\cdot}{OrtTN}{\cdot}{OrtTC} - k_{-1}{\cdot}{OrtT} – d_{OrtT}{\cdot}{OrtT} \end{align}$$

External

In order to estimate the killing effect on Salmonella of our system, this model is used to simulate the number of Salmonella.

Salmonella itself grows logically, and has a certain rate of conjugation with E.coli at each time point. According to the known literature, the growth rate of Salmonella is 10 /day, the maximum population density in the gut of chicken is 10000, and the conjugation rate we used is 0.4 / (cell · h).

There are Cl and [Cl]2 in the initial system, otherwise, part of the calculation will be meaningless. In the modeling, the cl content is 500, [Cl]2 content is 615000.Therefore, the model is obtained: $\frac{{\large d}{N}}{d t}=r{\cdot}{N}{\cdot}(1+{\frac{N}{K}})-{Joi}{\cdot}{Kr}{\cdot}{N}$

The simulation result is as follows:

pic3.The curve of the relation bewteen Salmonella's amount and time

It can be seen from the figure that when the binding rate is 0.4, the killing rate can reach 90%.However, it takes at least 12 hours to reach the expected killing rate, and the time to reach the balance is relatively long.
